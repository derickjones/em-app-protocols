Emergency Medicine App (EMA) is a clinical decision support tool built for emergency medicine. It gives physicians, APPs, nurses, and trainees instant, searchable access to their department's clinical protocols alongside the best open-access emergency medicine literature — all in one place, with answers in seconds.

Instead of navigating clunky hospital intranets or clicking through PDFs during a critical patient encounter, clinicians can ask a plain-language question and get a cited, actionable answer with the relevant flowcharts and algorithms displayed prominently.

HOW IT WORKS
• Protocol Ingestion — hospitals upload their protocols as PDFs; EMA extracts all text, images, flowcharts, and algorithms automatically
• AI-Powered Search — indexes everything so the most relevant content is retrieved in milliseconds
• Instant Answers — a concise, cited answer is synthesized from the retrieved content and streamed to you in real time
• Visual-First — flowcharts, algorithms, and protocol diagrams are displayed alongside every answer, with clickable citations back to the source PDF

WHO IT'S FOR
Emergency physicians, advanced practice providers (PAs, NPs), emergency department nurses, residents and fellows, and medical students on EM rotations.

BEYOND YOUR DEPARTMENT'S PROTOCOLS
EMA also searches across the best open-access emergency medicine resources — WikEM, PMC Open Access, LITFL, REBEL EM, and ALiEM — all used under Creative Commons licenses with full attribution.

Access to institution-specific protocols is gated: your department must approve your account before its protocols become searchable. General clinical reference content is available to any signed-in user.

Pilot notice: AI-generated content may contain errors and may change as we evaluate the tool. Always verify against primary sources and your own clinical judgment. This app is a reference aid for trained healthcare professionals — it is not a substitute for professional medical judgment and does not establish a provider-patient relationship.
